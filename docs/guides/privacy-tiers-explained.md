# Privacy Tiers Explained

## What Are Privacy Tiers?
Privacy tiers in BaseUSDP determine the size of the anonymity set for your transactions. A larger anonymity set means more users share the same deposit amount, making it harder to correlate deposits with withdrawals.

## Available Tiers

### Tier 1 — Micro (1 USDC)
- **Use case:** Testing, micro-payments, tips
- **Anonymity set:** Moderate
- **Best for:** New users exploring the protocol

### Tier 2 — Standard (10 USDC)
- **Use case:** Everyday payments, subscriptions
- **Anonymity set:** Large
- **Best for:** Regular privacy-conscious users

### Tier 3 — Enhanced (100 USDC)
- **Use case:** Business payments, larger transfers
- **Anonymity set:** Very large
- **Best for:** Users requiring strong privacy guarantees

### Tier 4 — Maximum (1,000 USDC)
- **Use case:** High-value transfers, treasury operations
- **Anonymity set:** Maximum
- **Best for:** Organizations and high-net-worth individuals

## How Anonymity Sets Work
When you deposit 10 USDC, your deposit joins a pool with every other 10 USDC deposit. When you later withdraw or transfer, the zero-knowledge proof demonstrates you are one of the depositors without revealing which one. The more depositors in your tier, the stronger your privacy.

## Choosing the Right Tier
- Consider the amount you need to transfer
- Higher tiers offer better privacy but require more capital
- You can split transactions across multiple deposits
- Monitor the anonymity set size on the dashboard before depositing

## Multiple Deposits
You can make multiple deposits across different tiers. Each deposit receives its own note and can be used independently for transfers.
